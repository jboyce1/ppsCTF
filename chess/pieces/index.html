Cybersecurity Chess Analogy

Pawn (1 point):
- Basic reconnaissance: Involves initial gathering of information about the target network.
- Scanning and enumeration: Identifying potential vulnerabilities and weaknesses in the target's perimeter.

Knight (3 points):
- Initial access: Gaining access to a user-level host or non-critical systems.
- Lateral movement: Moving within the network after initial access, without reaching a highly privileged level.

Bishop (5 points):
- Privileged access: Obtaining access to a privileged account or system.
- Data exfiltration: Successfully exfiltrating sensitive data from the target network.

Rook (7 points):
- Persistence: Establishing continued access within the target network.
- Escalation of privileges: Gaining higher access within the network.

Queen (10 points):
- Root access: Achieving the highest level of access within the target network.
- Complete control: Gaining control over critical systems or the entire network.

Checkmate (15 points):
- Complete compromise: Full control over the target network, including root access and data exfiltration.

Position (Bonus Points):
- Mate in 3 (5 bonus points): Achieving root privileges on an admin account or running C2 operations inside the adversary's network.
