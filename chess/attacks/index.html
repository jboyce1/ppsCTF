Cybersecurity Chess Analogy

Discovered Attack (Analogous to Privilege Escalation):
- Chess: A discovered attack occurs when one piece moves to reveal an attack from another piece behind it.
- Cybersecurity: Privilege escalation can be seen as a discovered attack. Attackers exploit vulnerabilities or weaknesses to reveal a hidden path to gain higher privileges within a system.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Privilege escalation (Tactic: Privilege Escalation, Techniques: T1078, T1068).

Overloading (Analogous to DDoS Attacks):
- Chess: Overloading in chess involves putting pressure on a piece or square with multiple threats, forcing the opponent to choose which threat to address.
- Cybersecurity: DDoS attacks can be seen as overloading. Attackers flood a target system with traffic, overwhelming it and making it difficult for legitimate users to access.
- MITRE ATT&CK: DDoS (Tactic: Impact, Technique: T1498).

Forks (Analogous to Credential Theft):
- Chess: A fork is a tactic where one piece attacks two or more opponent's pieces simultaneously.
- Cybersecurity: Credential theft can be compared to a fork-like tactic. Attackers steal credentials to gain access to multiple accounts or systems simultaneously.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Credential theft (Tactic: Credential Access, Techniques: T1003, T1555).

Skewers (Analogous to Data Exfiltration):
- Chess: A skewer is an attack that puts pressure on a more valuable piece by attacking a less valuable one in the same line.
- Cybersecurity: Data exfiltration can be compared to a skewer. Attackers target less secure or less valuable data to put pressure on organizations, forcing them to protect their most sensitive information.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Data exfiltration (Tactic: Exfiltration, Techniques: T1041, T1048).

Pin (Analogous to Defense Evasion):
- Chess: A pin occurs when a piece cannot move because it would expose a more valuable piece to capture.
- Cybersecurity: Defense evasion tactics can be seen as creating a pin. Attackers use techniques that restrict the movement or detection of security measures.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Defense evasion (Tactic: Defense Evasion, Techniques: Various).

Sacrifice (Analogous to Diversionary Attacks):
- Chess: Sacrifices involve giving up a piece to gain a positional or tactical advantage.
- Cybersecurity: Diversionary tactics can be likened to sacrifices. Attackers may intentionally expose or lose a less critical asset to divert attention from their primary objective.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Diversionary tactics (Tactic: Defense Evasion, Techniques: Various).

Zwischenzug (Analogous to Evasive Maneuvers):
- Chess: Zwischenzug is a chess tactic where an unexpected intermediate move disrupts the natural flow of the game.
- Cybersecurity: Evasive maneuvers can be compared to Zwischenzug. Attackers use unexpected tactics or techniques to disrupt defenders' responses.
- MITRE ATT&CK: Evasive tactics (Tactic: Defense Evasion, Techniques: Various).

Stalemate (Analogous to Defensive Success):
- Chess: Stalemate is a situation where the player cannot make any legal moves, resulting in a draw.
- Cybersecurity: Defensive success or incident response can be seen as a stalemate. Defenders prevent attackers from achieving their objectives, resulting in a neutral outcome.
- Cyber Kill Chain: Stalemate represents a successful defense that prevents the attacker from advancing through the Kill Chain phases.
